## Day 3 — The MBR

**Goal:** Understand the Monthly Business Review process end to end.

- [ ] Walk through the MBR agenda structure:
  - P&L actuals vs. forecast — prior month results vs. the forecast line
  - Variance deep-dive — drill to vendor and invoice level to explain what drove the variance
  - Headcount — current team vs. forecast; open roles, recent hires, departures
  - T&E detail — travel and expense review, flagging patterns or anomalies
  - Risks and opportunities — is the current forecast still accurate?
- [ ] **Key rule:** The MBR is not a reforecast. It is a diagnostic. Reforecasting happens quarterly. The MBR sharpens your view of risks and opportunities between reforecasts.
- [ ] Live walkthrough of `/mbr` on the Intranet with Colin — BU/department filter, P&L vs. forecast view, T&E drill-down

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Strategic Finance | Flow's name for FP&A |
| Forecast | Rolling 12-month forward plan. Always "Forecast," never "Budget." |
| F1 / F2 / F3 / F4 | Four annual forecasts: Dec, Mar, Jun, Sep |
| LRP | Long-Range Plan — 5-year financial model |
| MBR | Monthly Business Review — actuals vs. forecast review with each BU/department |
| Variance | Difference between actual results and the Forecast |
| Business Partner | Strategic Finance manager embedded in BU coverage |
| Coding | How Accounting classifies transactions in Intacct to departments and GL accounts |
| Run-Rate | December actual projected flat across 12 months — basis for FY2026 F1 forecast |
| Lease-Up | Period when a new property is filling units from zero to stabilized occupancy |
